# Social post design reference analysis

Ten professional reference creatives, inspected image by image. This document
records what each one *does*, what Cyflow may adapt, and what must not be copied.

**These references are direction, not designs to reproduce.** They belong to two
other brands (a purple SaaS "Peralytics" and a green personal brand
"Sajid Imran"). Cyflow must never render their colours, marks, names, or copy.
What transfers is the *structural grammar*, which is remarkably consistent
across both brands and is exactly what a generated system needs.

Reference images are inputs to design work only. They are never shown to users
and never appear in generated output.

## Per-image analysis

### 1. `1782806479229.jpg`

- **Category:** Stat / result highlight.
- **Layout:** Dark near-black field with a soft purple radial glow in the upper
  right. Faint square grid across the whole canvas. Logo top-left, outlined
  status pill top-right. A rule-plus-label eyebrow ("— RESULT"). An enormous
  "50%" occupying roughly the middle third. A plain label under it. A small
  bar-chart glyph with "↑ trending up". Footer split left/right: dotted domain,
  and "SEE FULL CASE →".
- **Typography:** One hero number at perhaps 8x the body size, in a light
  vertical gradient. The label under it is regular weight, not bold — it defers
  to the number. Eyebrow and footer are letterspaced uppercase at ~2% of the
  canvas height.
- **Colour:** ~85% dark canvas, purple accents, one pale hero. Three colours
  total, in very unequal proportion.
- **Density:** Low. One idea.
- **Principles:** The number *is* the design; everything else shrinks to support
  it. A micro-detail ("↑ trending up") adds credibility without clutter. The
  footer is a two-sided lockup, not a centred line.
- **Cyflow may adapt:** hero-number scale; the deferential label; the eyebrow
  rule; the split footer; the supporting micro-detail.
- **Do not copy:** the purple palette, the "P" mark, the wordmark, "aiseocompany.com",
  the specific 50% claim.

### 2. `1782892833955.jpg`

- **Category:** Comparison / myth vs reality.
- **Layout:** Pale lavender wash. Header row (logo left, filled yellow pill
  right) separated by a hairline. Eyebrow "— EVOLUTION". One-line headline. Two
  equal cards side by side. A circular "VS" badge straddling the gap. A "★ NEW
  WAY" tab attached to the top edge of the right card. Footer: hairline, phrase
  left, domain right.
- **Typography:** Headline mixes black text with one accent-coloured phrase
  ("Local SEO"). Column labels are small letterspaced caps. Row text is medium
  weight, separated by hairlines.
- **Colour:** Near-white ground; purple used for the favoured column's border,
  label chip, tab and headline accent; a muted red-pink dot only on the "OLD"
  label; yellow only on the badge.
- **Density:** Medium. 3 rows per side.
- **Principles:** **The structure carries the argument.** The preferred option is
  marked by border, tab, checkmark and colour — never by saying "better". The
  rejected option is struck through and greyed. The "VS" badge is a real
  connector sitting in the gutter, not decoration.
- **Cyflow may adapt:** two-card comparison; a favoured column marked by border +
  label chip; strikethrough for the discarded option; a connector badge; a tab
  attached to a card edge.
- **Do not copy:** the palette, "THE SHIFT"/"NEW WAY" wording, the brand mark.

### 3. `1782979262222.jpg`

- **Category:** Checklist / numbered cheatsheet.
- **Layout:** Pale wash. Header + hairline. Eyebrow "— TIP". A two-part headline:
  black line, then an accent-coloured word on its own line at a larger size.
  Five white rows with soft shadows. Each row: a rounded-square numbered chip,
  a bold title, and a grey detail line beneath.
- **Typography:** Row titles bold; row details a smaller grey line. **Every row
  carries two lines**, which is what makes the list useful rather than a list of
  labels.
- **Colour:** Near-white ground, purple chips, black text, grey detail.
- **Density:** High, and the rows fill the canvas. No empty void.
- **Principles:** A checklist row is *title + detail*, not a bare phrase. Five
  rows fill a 1080 square comfortably. The numbered chip gives the eye a rail to
  run down.
- **Cyflow may adapt:** numbered chips; row = title + detail; five rows filling
  the field; the accent word on its own headline line.
- **Do not copy:** the palette, the mark, "Save this playbook".

### 4. `1784023338666.jpg`

- **Category:** Editorial insight on a brand field.
- **Layout:** Full-bleed diagonal gradient (dark indigo top-left to vivid purple
  bottom-right). Faint grid. A large ghosted quote glyph in the upper right at
  very low opacity. Logo top-left, filled "✦ INSIGHT" pill top-right. Eyebrow
  rule + label. Two-line headline. One supporting line. Footer: hairline, an
  avatar + two-line author lockup left, domain right.
- **Typography:** Headline ~2 lines, heavy, tight leading. **Emphasis varies
  inside the headline**: "for" is dimmed, "SEO success" is a paler tint.
- **Colour:** One brand gradient plus white ink at three opacities (100% / ~75% /
  ~45%). Effectively a one-colour design.
- **Density:** Low. Roughly the lower 40% is empty.
- **Principles:** **A gradient field makes empty space read as intentional.** The
  same emptiness on a flat fill would look unfinished. Ink opacity, not extra
  colours, creates hierarchy. The ghosted glyph is a watermark tied to the
  content type, not a blob.
- **Cyflow may adapt:** brand-gradient field; opacity-based hierarchy; emphasis
  spans inside a headline; an author/footer lockup; a low-opacity watermark that
  means something.
- **Do not copy:** the purple, the quote glyph as a literal asset, the team name.

### 5. `1784023354630.jpg`

- **Category:** Editorial insight (same family as #4).
- **Layout:** Identical grammar; a three-line headline instead of two.
- **Typography:** "Geo-targeting can / redefine **your local SEO** / impact." The
  accent tint spans mid-sentence. The final line is a single word with a full
  stop.
- **Note — a deliberate counter-example:** the one-word final line ("impact.")
  is normally a defect, and Cyflow's rule is to reduce size before allowing one.
  Here it reads as a rhetorical stop because the word is short, punctuated, and
  the line above is long. Cyflow should keep the rule: a *generator* cannot
  judge when the exception applies, so it must not be given the licence.
- **Colour / density:** As #4.
- **Cyflow may adapt:** the same family; three lines as the hard maximum.
- **Do not copy:** palette, marks, copy.

### 6. `1784102665739.jpg`

- **Category:** Comparison (same family as #2).

### 7. `Adapt for AI-driven Content Success.jpg`

## Combined analysis

### Repeated visual principles

Across ten images and two unrelated brands, the same grammar recurs:

1. **A fixed header band.** Brand mark top-left, a category pill top-right.
   Always. It never moves and never repeats elsewhere.
2. **An eyebrow made of a short rule**, optionally followed by a letterspaced
   uppercase label. The rule alone is enough (#7–#10).
3. **A headline that is the largest thing on the canvas**, 2 lines by default,
   3 at most, with tight leading and a heavy weight.
4. **One emphasis device inside the headline** — an accent-coloured span or a
   dimmed word. Never more than one idea highlighted.
5. **A single content block** below the headline: a number, a list, or two
   columns. Never two competing blocks.
6. **A hairline above the footer**, then a two-sided footer lockup: identity on
   the left, a link or mark on the right.
7. **Two colours, in very unequal proportion.** A field colour and an ink. A
   third colour appears only as a small chip, if at all.
8. **Ink opacity creates hierarchy**, not extra hues: 100% for the headline,
   ~75% for support, ~45% for the eyebrow and footer.
9. **A faint grid or gradient occupies "empty" space.** Emptiness is never flat;
   that is what stops it reading as unfinished.
10. **Nothing is decorative.** Every mark is a rule, a chip, a tick, a connector,
    a watermark tied to the content type, or type itself. There is not one
    circle, blob or scatter of shapes anywhere in ten professional references.

### Recommended Cyflow design families

The references collapse into four structures. Cyflow's families should map onto
them rather than proliferate:

| Family | From | Carries |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Editorial insight** | #4, #5 | brand-gradient field, 2–3 line headline, one support line |
| **Light editorial** | #2, #3 headers | pale wash, brand rail/accent, one emphasis span |
| **Checklist guide** | #3, #7–#10 | 3–5 rows; chip + title (+ optional detail) |
| **Comparison cards** | #2, #6 | two cards, favoured side marked, connector badge |
| **Stat highlight** | #1 | hero number, deferential label, credibility micro-detail |

Service-authority and local-insight are Cyflow-specific extensions of *editorial
insight* — they should inherit its grammar rather than invent a new one.

### Recommended typography hierarchy

Expressed against a 1080 square:

| Role | Size | Weight | Case | Opacity |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hero number (stat only) | 190–230px | 800 | — | 100% |
| Headline | 52–108px, by length | 700–800 | sentence | 100% |
| Headline emphasis span | same | same | same | accent colour or ~60% |
| Row / list title | 26–32px | 600–700 | sentence | 100% |
| Row detail | 20–24px | 400 | sentence | ~60% |
| Support line | 27–30px | 400 | sentence | ~75% |
| Eyebrow / badge | 18–22px | 700 | UPPER, .14–.22em | accent |
| Footer | 19–22px | 600 | sentence | ~70% |

Leading tightens as size grows (0.98–1.14). Tracking goes slightly negative on
the headline (-0.01 to -0.03em) and strongly positive on eyebrows.

### Recommended spacing system

- Canvas padding: **72–96px** (7–9% of the edge). Never less than 64.
- Header to eyebrow: ~2 padding units. Eyebrow to headline: ~24px.
- Headline to support: ~24px. Support to content block: ~40px.
- Rows: 12–18px apart; row padding 18–26px depending on count.
- Hairline sits ~20px above the footer lockup.
- Reserve the **bottom ~12%** for the footer. It anchors the composition; without
  it the canvas floats.

### Recommended post-copy and image relationship

The references make the division explicit:

- The **image headline is a claim**, 4–10 words, and it is the *whole* of the
  visual argument. It is not a title for the copy.
- The **content block is the evidence** — the list, the columns, the number.
- The **post copy carries what will not fit**: the reasoning, the caveats, the
  next step. It must *develop* the headline, never restate it.
- If the headline and the copy say the same thing, one of them is redundant. A
  reader who sees only the image should get a complete thought; a reader who
  reads the copy should get more, not the same again.

### How exact business brand colours should be introduced

Reference #7–#10 (green + black) versus #1–#6 (purple + white) prove the rule:
**the palette is a variable; the grammar is not.**

For a saved profile, assign roles rather than mutating hexes:

1. **Field** = the darkest or most dominant saved colour, used full-bleed or as a
   large panel, ideally with a subtle gradient toward one corner.
2. **Ink** = whichever of near-black or white actually passes contrast on that
   field. Chosen for the field, never fixed to a mode.
3. **Accent** = the most chromatic saved colour. Reserved for the eyebrow rule,
   the badge outline, one headline span, the CTA fill, and footer dots. Small
   area only.
4. **Support** = the next saved colour. Rarer still: a divider, a second chip.
5. **Neutrals** = white and near-black are always permissible as ink and surface.

Proportion target, from the references: roughly **80% field / 15% ink /
5% accent**. Never three colours at equal weight.

A colour the business did not save must never appear. If a saved colour cannot
carry legible ink, adjust its *lightness only*, keep its hue, and record that the
adjustment happened.
